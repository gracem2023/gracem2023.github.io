About Me

At the young age of eleven, I was shipped off to boarding school. My foster parents rejoiced at the riddance of my troublesome burden, but even at school it seemed I was a magnet for mischief. As trials and tribulations presented themselves, I tested the trust of true friendships, wept at the death of my only true loved one, and spent lots of time riding a broomstick. Until one day I turned the last page of Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows, and I was thrust back into the real world, where I had no known magical powers. So every night, I curiously explored different realms, shapeshifting from a time traveler to a war hero to the lead singer of a world famous rock band, pondering how I would make my mark on the world.

While I dream of one day drafting my own novel, I wield a paint brush as my own wand, illuminating stories through visual arts. Since my youth, painting has come naturally. In elementary school, I started a business selling portraits of family photographs that clients submitted. As I branched out into the world with the confidence to spark random conversations, I discovered that stories rivaling those of the greatest novels surround me everyday. In high school, I continued this passion of highlighting significant characters in my communities through my AP 2-D Art and Design portfolio, expressing individuals’ hopes, desires, and desperations via the color wheel. As the time came to apply to college, I realized that my true passion is people.

In 2020, I was honored to be accepted into Georgia Tech’s renowned Scheller College of Business. However, after my first semester of coursework, I found that the once dreaded required coding class had become my favorite course, reigniting my love for analytic problem-solving. Curiosity drove me to research how I could advance my computer literacy, and I found the Computational Media major. This major allows me to combine my interest in computer science with the artistic storytelling that I have grown up loving, but I could not completely give up the business segment of my degree. Thus, I am excited to be a part of the Denning Technology and Management Minor to further my connections between technology and the business world. As I advance my education with a concentration in Interaction Design and People, I would like to pursue a career in UX Design, where I can continue to apply insights about people to web and software design.
